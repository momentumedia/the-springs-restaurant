An honest read of the current site.

We pulled the current site through a discoverability, design, and conversion lens. Nine findings. We're careful to flag anything estimated versus verified. Nothing below is invented for effect.

Critical

Three things hurting you right now.

  • Critical

    The menu is a picture, not a page.

    Search engines and AI assistants cannot read your menu. Guests searching "does The Springs have vegetarian mains" get nothing back, because the words aren't on the page.

    Route:       /menus
Content:     JPG files  (Lunch Menu, Dinner Menu, Wine Dessert Menu)
Readable by: Humans on a screen, at full resolution
Readable by: Google · partially (OCR is unreliable on menus)
Readable by: ChatGPT Search / Perplexity / Claude · no
Estimated impact: menu-specific queries return zero recommendations
  • Critical

    You have two websites, and guests can't tell which is real.

    Your brand lives at thespringsrestaurant.com. Your bookings and event set menus live at thespringsrestaurant.square.site. A guest who starts on one ends up bouncing to the other and back. Staff edits in two places.

    Surface 1:   thespringsrestaurant.com         (brand · events · blog)
Surface 2:   thespringsrestaurant.square.site  (bookings · set menus · shop)
Shared:     Nothing automatically. Hours, pricing, menus are duplicated by hand.
Typical consequence: stale pricing, divergent hours, split analytics
  • Critical

    AI search tools get locked out at the door.

    When we tried to read your pages automatically, most returned a block page. That's the same experience ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, and Google's AI summaries get when they try to learn about you.

    Probed:       /, /menus, /events, /weddings, /contact
Responses:    401 and 403 on most non-root pages
Verified:     Yes, during this audit
Estimated impact: reduced presence in AI-generated restaurant recommendations
High

Three things costing you bookings.

  • High

    Event pricing is hidden on a second domain.

    You have clear tiers: Lunch from $65, Dinner from $79 to $115. Couples planning a wedding never see those numbers on your main site, so they assume they're more expensive than they are, or they leave.

    Main site events pages:  No prices published
Square site set menus:   Seven tiers, publicly priced
Click path to price:      events.thespringsrestaurant.com → exit → Google → Square → menu image
Estimated impact: weddings that never submit an inquiry
  • High

    Your hours are complicated. The site doesn't make them easy.

    Thursday and Friday have split lunch and dinner service. Other days open at 4:30pm. That's not a bug, that's real. But the hours are body text only, not structured data Google can consume.

    Element:     Plain paragraph on home page
Schema:      No schema.org/OpeningHoursSpecification markup detected
Verified:    Home page HTML scanned this audit
Estimated impact: Google Business Profile, Maps, Siri may show incomplete hours
  • High

    Image-weight is throttling mobile load.

    When your menu is a JPG and your homepage leans on full-resolution photos, mobile load time suffers. On a weak 4G connection, the menu page can feel slow enough that guests give up.

    Menu page assets:   Full-resolution JPGs, no responsive srcset
Image format:       JPG where WebP or AVIF would cut weight 40 to 60 percent
Lazy loading:       Inconsistent (verified: some images load eagerly)
Typical LCP on 4G:  3.5 to 6 seconds  (estimated from asset weight)
Medium

Three things holding the brand back.

  • Medium

    The template doesn't match the room.

    You're a casual fine-dining bistro in a restored church. The current layout feels like a generic restaurant template. The building tells a story. The site should echo it.

    Observed:    Builder-style layout, limited typographic contrast
Expected:    Editorial rhythm, generous whitespace, serif display, hero imagery
Impact:      Brand perception mismatch · guests expect a different experience than the site implies
  • Medium

    The reserve button stops following the guest.

    As a guest scrolls and reads, the reservation button disappears into the nav. A sticky CTA on scroll recovers the bookings that happen in the second or third scroll impulse.

    Observed:   Reservation link in top nav only
Missing:    Sticky bottom CTA on mobile · hero button · footer CTA
Typical impact: 8 to 15 percent fewer booking clicks than a sticky-CTA equivalent
  • Medium

    The photography deserves a bigger stage.

    The plates and the room photograph well. They're in thumbnails when they could be in a full-bleed hero or a lightboxed gallery.

    Assets observed:  Quality dish photography, venue shots, wedding shots
Presentation:     Small tiles, no lightbox, no Instagram integration
Opportunity:      Full-bleed hero, masonry gallery, IG embed
What's working

Strong bones, real assets, and a name worth keeping.

None of the above is meant to suggest nothing is working. A few things are working very well, and the rebuild keeps them.

  • The name and the building

    "The Springs" · a restored historic church. That's a brand you don't rewrite. We keep it front and center.

  • Genuine photography

    The plates are real. The weddings are real. The room is real. We build the new site around those assets, not stock.

  • Focused positioning

    "Casual fine-dining bistro + event venue" is a clear two-part story. Guests understand it on first pass.
