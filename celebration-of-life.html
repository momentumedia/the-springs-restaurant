Celebration of life · London, Ontario

A quiet table. A warm meal. A team that stays out of the way.

When a family needs a space to gather after a loss, we try to make the rest easy. Any day, any time, any size. No venue fee. No deposit. No minimum spend. No restrictions.

What we offer

A quiet space, warm food, a team that doesn't hover.

A celebration of life calls for a different kind of room. The building was a church, and it still carries that quiet. We've hosted many families. We know how to hold the room, and how to stay out of the way.

Reach out any day, any hour, and we'll find a way to fit your date. Same-week bookings are possible when families need the space quickly. No paperwork before a conversation.

What we don't do

No barriers in the way of your day.

We don't charge a venue fee. We don't ask for a deposit. We don't hold you to a minimum spend or a minimum guest count. We don't add rules to a day that doesn't need any more. These are not things we add when a family is already carrying a lot.

  • Any hour

    Weekday afternoons. Evening receptions. Any day, including holidays.

  • No venue fee

    No rental line. Pay for food and drink, nothing on top.

  • No deposit

    Call and confirm. We'll put the date on the calendar.

  • No minimum spend

    Small family gathering or a room of 80. Both are fine.

  • No restrictions

    Any format, any pacing. A slideshow, a eulogy, a long silence. However your family needs it.

Food

Hors-d'oeuvre reception or a seated meal.

Most families choose an hors-d'oeuvre reception. It lets guests move, greet, and eat at their own pace. A seated lunch or dinner also works when the room prefers it. We'll walk through the options with you.

  • Two hors-d'oeuvre selection tiers
  • Lunch set menus at $65 or $75 per person
  • Dinner set menus from $79 to $115 per person
  • Coffee and tea service available
  • Still and sparkling on hand
Ambiance

A restored church, softly set.

Exposed brick, arched windows, pitched wood ceilings, soft light. The room can be dressed up with florals or kept simple and plain. Candlelight at night. Quiet acoustics.

  • Room dressed simply by default
  • Florals and decor available on request
  • AV for a slideshow or memorial reel
  • Microphone for eulogies and tributes
Room size

Small and close, or full and seated.

The private room holds up to 35 around a long table, which works for close family. The main hall holds 85 seated, or more standing for a reception. Both rooms combined for larger services.

  • Private room (35) for immediate family
  • Main hall (85) for a larger community
  • Combined for full-congregation gatherings
  • Outdoor tent for weather-permitting receptions
FAQ

Quick answers for a hard week.

  • Can you accommodate on short notice?

    Yes. Same-week bookings are possible for families who need the space quickly. Call us.

  • Is the venue appropriate for a quiet gathering?

    Yes. The building was a church and still carries that quiet. Rooms at multiple sizes so the space feels held, not empty.

  • Do you have AV for a slideshow or memorial reel?

    Projection and screen available. We'll test the setup with you ahead of the day.

  • What capacity can you hold?

    Private room up to 35. Main hall up to 85. Both combined for larger services.

  • Is there a venue fee or deposit?

    No. No venue fee. No deposit. No minimum spend.

  • What food works best?

    Most families choose an hors-d'oeuvre reception so guests can move and greet. A seated meal also works.

  • Is the venue accessible?

    Ground-level entry, wheelchair accessible, complimentary on-site parking.
